WarpWallet

What is WarpWallet?

WarpWallet is a deterministic bitcoin wallet generator. You never have to save or store your bitcoin wallet anywhere -- online or offline. When you want to extract the BTC (and import to an online wallet), just re-generate with this page.

You can save this self-contained HTML file and access it wherever you like, even on an air-gapped computer. Heck, email it to 10 friends if you're afraid it will go away. All the JS, CSS, and images are embedded.

This is not an original idea. bitaddress.org's brainwallet is our inspiration.

WarpWallet adds two improvements: (1) WarpWallet uses scrypt to make address generation both memory and time-intensive. And (2) you can salt your passphrase with your email address. This is trivial for you to remember, so why not? It is optional of course.

If you're a programmer and want to reproduce WarpWallet's output, it's simply: 

  s1 = scrypt(key=passphrase, salt=salt, N=2^18, r=1, p=8, dkLen=32)
  s2 = pbkdf2(key=s1, salt=salt, c=2^16, dkLen=32)
  seed = s1^s2
  keypair = generate_bitcoin_keypair(seed)

WarpWallet Challenge

The following challenges are designed to test the safety of WarpWallet. We put our own bitcoins in for the challenge. We expect the first 3 challenges to go quickly. If challenges 4 and 5 fall, WarpWallet is not safe.

Challenge 1
  • reward: 0.25 BTC
  • hint: This passphrase is the username of the someone in the Hacker News top 100 karma list, as of November 19, 2013. However, we have changed the case on his/her name and reordered the characters. For example, 'btilly' might be 'YiLLtB'.
  • wallet: 1F1k4fX...
  • status: unbroken
Challenge 2
  • reward: 0.5 BTC
  • hint: this passphrase is 4 random alphanumeric characters, such as 'hb2A'.
  • wallet: 1F1k4fX...
  • status: unbroken
Challenge 3
  • reward: 0.5 BTC
  • hint: this passphrase is 5 random alphanumeric characters, such as '89GPx'.
  • wallet: 1F1k4fX...
  • status: unbroken

The first couple levels should go quickly. A passphrase with only 4 characters and no salt is pretty simple.
